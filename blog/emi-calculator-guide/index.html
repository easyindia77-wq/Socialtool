What is EMI?

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed amount that a borrower pays every month to repay a loan. It includes both the principal amount and the interest charged by the lender. EMI ensures that the loan is repaid completely over a specified period.

Understanding EMI is very important before taking any loan because it directly affects your monthly budget and financial stability.

EMI Formula Explained

The EMI is calculated using a standard mathematical formula:

EMI = [P × R × (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N – 1]

Where:

  • P = Loan Amount
  • R = Monthly Interest Rate
  • N = Number of Monthly Installments
